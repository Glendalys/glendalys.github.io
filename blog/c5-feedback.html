Give and Take!

Relections on Peer Pairing through DBC

January 24, 2015

Pairing

• What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges?

I am a loner and introverted at heart so the idea of learning by pairing was originally freighting. Since starting to pair I have had the best experiences when roles are define and time blocking is put into place. I also find it very helpful to prepare a bit before the pairing and make sure I understand what the challenge is and what steps are need to fulfill the desired outcome. I also make an effort to set an intension before starting. For example, I want to drive and navigate for at least 30mins, making sure to check-in with my pair, ask questions if I’m not following, listen to my pairs needs and want and communicate mine.

• Was something particularly fun or rewarding?

During one pairing session I had I was able to help with concepts and the challenge more than usual because I had done it earlier in the week and set up that session as a review. It was nice to know something and help explain some concepts to someone.

• Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

I find it difficult when someone know the material and powers through it doesn’t check in with me to make sure I understand. I especially find it frustrating when that same person when asked can’t explain to me what they are doing. I feel that if you are doing something and you can’t explain it then maybe you shouldn’t be using it because you obviously don’t have a full understanding.

Feedback

• How did you feel when you read your feedback?

I am amazed that anyone thought I helped him or her technically. I generally feel like I get more technically then I give during parings.

• Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

The feedback for the most part confirm my thoughts on what areas I need improvement.

• Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

I am going to time-box and drive more. I am also going to be more selective of who I pair with because I feel that some people are more suited for my style of learning.

• How was it to write feedback?

It was hard sometimes to give a person honest constructive feedback and still be kind.

Overall, what do you think of using pairing and feedback to guide your learning?

I think when it’s a good pairing I learn a lot about the material and when it’s a not so great pairing I learn a lot about how I learn, how to communicate and listen to my pair. So either the way I am learning something which is always good.
